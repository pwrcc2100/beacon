# Beacon Index: Summary

## Overview

**Beacon Index** is a governance instrument designed for boards and executives to measure and manage psychosocial risk and psychological safety. It provides governance-ready visibility into the structural drivers of psychological safety, enabling early, targeted leadership action that strengthens organisational stability.

## The Problem It Solves

Most organisations detect psychosocial risk late—after complaints, conflict, or escalation. The core issue is visibility, not intent. Traditional approaches suffer from:

- **Lag Indicators**: Risk becomes visible only after impacting people, teams, or performance
- **Unstructured Measurement**: Generic sentiment data is difficult to translate into governance decisions
- **Accountability Risk**: Without consistent oversight, leaders cannot demonstrate disciplined monitoring

## How It Works

Beacon Index uses a simple, efficient assessment process:

1. Employee scans QR code or accesses link
2. Answers five calibrated questions
3. Composite 0–100 score generated
4. Risk band classification assigned
5. Report available for immediate analysis

**Key Metrics:**
- **5 Questions** total
- **<2 minutes** completion time
- **0–100** risk score range
- **Real-time dashboard** insights

## Five Structural Drivers

The assessment measures five core domains that drive psychological safety:

1. **Experience** — Early signal of emerging systemic strain
2. **Workload & Resourcing** — Demand and capacity balance across teams
3. **Psychological Safety** — Confidence to speak up without negative consequence
4. **Leadership & Support** — Practical and behavioural support leaders provide under pressure
5. **Clarity & Direction** — Priority, expectation, and decision-boundary alignment

## Risk Framework

Defined risk bands support consistent interpretation and escalation:

- **≥ 80** — Low Psychosocial Risk Exposure (Healthy operating conditions, standard monitoring)
- **70–79** — Within Psychosocial Risk Tolerance (Acceptable exposure range, trend monitoring required)
- **60–69** — Emerging Psychosocial Risk Exposure (Early strain indicators, targeted review recommended)
- **< 60** — Elevated Psychosocial Risk Exposure (Sustained systemic strain, structured intervention required)

## Target Audiences

### Executive Leadership
Provides clear overall risk profile and structured oversight at board and executive level.

### HR & People Teams
Translates broad sentiment data into actionable structural insight highlighting specific drivers.

### Safety & Risk
Embeds repeatable psychosocial risk oversight into governance rhythm, similar to physical hazard monitoring.

### Operations Leaders
Surfaces workload, clarity, and leadership conditions requiring attention, linking daily work design to risk outcomes.

## Governance Benefits

- **Documented Oversight**: Traceable, auditable records demonstrating active monitoring
- **Early Strain Visibility**: Weekly structural signals identify emerging pressure before harm occurs
- **Defensible Executive Reporting**: Calibrated scoring enables consistent board-level reporting aligned to recognised frameworks
- **Cross-Functional Accountability**: Shared structural model supports coordinated responsibility across teams
- **ISO 45003 Compliant**: Aligned with international psychosocial risk management standards

## Key Distinction

**Not another wellbeing survey.** Beacon Index is designed specifically for governance oversight, providing:
- Clear overall risk profile (not just sentiment)
- Ongoing structural monitoring (not episodic measurement)
- Focused psychosocial risk monitoring (not broad culture reporting)

Engagement tools help organisations understand how people feel. Beacon Index helps leaders understand where structural risk may require attention.
